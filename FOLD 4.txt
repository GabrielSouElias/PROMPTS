# FOLD 4 — CTA INTERSTITIAL | Adega Própria Landing Page

---

## Role & Design Philosophy

You are a senior UI designer and frontend developer building the **conversion interstitial** of the Adega Própria landing page — the unignorable pause where three folds of emotional accumulation (hero aspiration → video persuasion → manifesto soul) collapse into a single question: *now?*

This is the first CTA since Fold 1. That gap is architectural. The visitor has traveled a long way since they last saw a button, and Fold 4 exists to honor that distance — to stop the page, demand a decision, and provide the elegant mechanism for saying yes. It is **the conversion cut**: the moment the page shifts register from storytelling to invitation.

Build a premium, **wine-dark** interstitial that functions like an editorial pull quote scaled to room-filling presence. A giant Fraunces declaration, centered, weighted like it was carved into cellar stone. A single gold italic word carrying the psychological thesis of the entire page. One CTA. One trust signal. Nothing else. This fold is a closed door with a gold handle — the visitor opens it or keeps scrolling.

**This is the darkest fold on the page.** Every prior fold has operated in the same atmospheric range: wine-black dominance with subtle wine-red and clay washes. Fold 4 pushes deeper — `#1A0F10` becomes near-total shadow at the edges, `#A62C21` comes forward as a deliberate atmospheric presence (still within the Master's 0.04–0.10 opacity ceiling, still disciplined), and the signature gold whispers in exactly three places: the italic word, the CTA pill, and a **vertical gold hairline that draws from ceiling to floor behind the headline** — a visual axis signaling the page is physically centering on this decision.

**The darkness is wine-red, not green.** Canvas is `#1A0F10`. Shadow layer is `#2A1416`. Warm depths are `#8C3D20` and `#A62C21`. Gold and beige appear only in the three loci above. Never green. Never bright. Never loud. The fold must feel like walking from the candlelit cellar into its deepest chamber, where the only warmth is the bottle you're about to uncork.

**Reference energy for this specific fold:**
- **Tenuta Luce** — the editorial pull quote approach, italic serif at declarative scale
- **Pasqua** — the cinematic interruption, a section that stops the visitor mid-scroll
- **Kettmeir** — typographic restraint, generous darkness around the type

**Design mandates (non-negotiable):**
- No image. No split layout. No card. No countdown timer or scarcity tactic (destroys luxury).
- No emoji icons. No generic gradients. No inline styles (except for complex radial gradients Tailwind cannot express).
- No secondary "learn more" ghost button. Exactly one CTA.
- Typography must be intentional — continue the Fraunces + DM Sans pairing established in Folds 1, 2, 3.
- The Fraunces headline in this fold is **the largest type on the entire landing page**. It must earn that scale by being brief and emotionally loaded.
- Mobile-first: design for 375px, then enhance for desktop.
- The fold must pass the "screenshot test" — captured as a still, it should read as a luxury wine brand manifesto, not a conversion section.

This is the fold's theatrical move — a **1px vertical gold line** positioned at horizontal center, running from the top of the headline zone to the bottom of the trust signal. It visualizes the page axis the content hangs on. On entrance, it draws from top to bottom over 1.4 seconds.

- `absolute top-[22%] lg:top-[20%] left-1/2 -translate-x-1/2 w-px z-[5]`

- Use inline `top`/`bottom` for precise control, or compute height explicitly

### Visual Treatment

Not a solid line. A **gradient line with soft ends** — fades into the darkness at top and bottom, strongest at the center:


The center peak opacity of `0.35` feels gold but stays disciplined. The soft ends prevent it from feeling like a harsh ruler dividing the fold.

### Behind the Axis — Subtle Glow

Behind the hairline, a very faint vertical gold glow (the "softener"):

- No animation — it's ambient

On mobile, the axis is subtler — reduced height range and lower peak opacity:

### `prefers-reduced-motion`

When `true`: render the axis at final state (`scaleY: 1, opacity: 1`) immediately. Skip the draw-down animation.

## Animation & Motion Specification

### Scroll-Triggered Pattern

Use Framer Motion's `useInView` on a section-level ref:

```jsx
const sectionRef = useRef(null);
const isInView = useInView(sectionRef, { once: true, amount: 0.35 });
```

`amount: 0.35` — triggers when 35% of the fold is in view. Later than Folds 2/3 because this fold is SHORTER (`min-h-[85vh]` vs. `min-h-screen`), so 35% ensures the visitor has genuinely arrived, not just glimpsed the top.

### Entrance Choreography

This is the most deliberate, slowest-paced entrance of any fold — the interstitial EARNS attention, it doesn't fade in quickly.

| Element | Initial | Animate (on inView) | Duration | Delay | Easing |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vertical axis hairline | `scaleY: 0, opacity: 0` | `scaleY: 1, opacity: 1` | 1400ms | 200ms | `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` |
| Eyebrow group (tick + label) | `opacity: 0, y: 12` | `opacity: 1, y: 0` | 700ms | 400ms | `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` |
| Giant headline | `opacity: 0, y: 50` | `opacity: 1, y: 0` | 1200ms | 700ms | `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` |
| Supporting line | `opacity: 0, y: 25` | `opacity: 1, y: 0` | 900ms | 1100ms | `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` |
| CTA pill | `opacity: 0, y: 20, scale: 0.95` | `opacity: 1, y: 0, scale: 1` | 900ms | 1400ms | `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` |
| Trust micro-text | `opacity: 0` | `opacity: 1` | 700ms | 1700ms | `easeOut` |

**Total choreography runtime: ~2.4 seconds.** This is longer than the hero's ~1.5s because interstitials should feel composed, not snappy. The visitor has just scrolled through a manifesto; Fold 4 lets them settle before presenting the decision.

**Why the axis animates first:** The hairline drawing from top to bottom physically "opens" the fold — it's the ceremonial gesture that says "this section is establishing itself." Every subsequent element appears within the frame the axis has drawn.

**Why the headline has a 700ms delay:** It gives the axis time to traverse most of its height before the headline settles in. The two animations overlap but the axis leads. This creates the feeling of the fold building itself around the declaration.

### Mobile vs. Desktop Motion

- **Mobile (`< 1024px`):** Opacity-only fades on all entrances (remove `y` translations). Keep the axis `scaleY` animation (it's vertical and doesn't cause layout shift). Keep stagger timing.
- **Desktop (`lg:+`):** Full choreography as specified.
- **`prefers-reduced-motion`:** Skip all entrance animations (render final state), skip axis draw-down (render at `scaleY: 1`), skip CTA shimmer animation.

### Framer Motion Implementation Pattern

```jsx
const prefersReducedMotion = typeof window !== 'undefined' 
  ? window.matchMedia('(prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)').matches 
  : false;

const [isMobile, setIsMobile] = useState(true);

useEffect(() => {
  const check = () => setIsMobile(window.innerWidth < 1024);
  check();
  window.addEventListener('resize', check);
  return () => window.removeEventListener('resize', check);
}, []);

// Example element:

```

Setting `initial={false}` under reduced motion skips animation entirely.

### CTA Shimmer Edge (subtlety is the point)

The shimmer is a **2.4-second infinite horizontal gradient sweep** that only becomes visible on hover. When inactive, the CTA is a calm solid gold pill. When hovered, a ghost of light travels its edge. The effect should be SO subtle that a casual viewer notices it subliminally rather than seeing it outright. If the effect is obvious, reduce the gradient opacity.

```css
@keyframes shimmer {
  0% { background-position: -100% 0; }
  100% { background-position: 200% 0; }
}
```

Apply via `animation: shimmer 2.4s ease-in-out infinite` on the absolute-positioned shimmer layer inside the pill. Set `mix-blend-mode: overlay` so the shimmer interacts with the gold beneath rather than overlaying it flatly.
