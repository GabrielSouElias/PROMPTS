# FOLD 2 — VIDEO DE VENDAS | Adega Própria Landing Page

---

## Role & Design Philosophy

You are a senior UI designer and frontend developer building the **sales video fold** of the Adega Própria landing page. This fold has one job: **present a video in a way that feels cinematic, intentional, and premium — then get out of the way and let the video sell.**

This is NOT a generic YouTube embed dropped into a section. This is NOT a modal launcher. This is a **private screening room experience** — the visitor should feel like they've been invited to watch something rare. The video frame itself is a design object: a wine-dark vessel that contains the persuasion.

There is no CTA in this fold. The video itself IS the conversion mechanism. CTAs live in Folds 1, 4, 6, 8, and 9. The job of Fold 2 is to present the video with enough premium framing that the visitor chooses to press play — and once they press play, the design disappears and the message takes over.

**The darkness is wine-red, not green.** Canvas is `#1A0F10`. The video frame sits within this darkness like a lit painting in a gallery. Gold appears only in the subtle ring around the frame, the small play button, and restrained text accents. Never green. Never bright. Never loud.

**Reference energy for this specific fold:**
- **Pasqua** — the cinematic "enter the experience" framing, the sense that pressing play is entering a film
- **Kettmeir** — editorial restraint, generous whitespace around key content moments
- **Tenuta Luce** — refined frames with gold hairline details

**Design mandates (non-negotiable):**
- No emoji icons. No generic gradients. No inline styles (except for complex radial gradients Tailwind cannot express).
- No bright play button. No oversized UI. The frame is the hero; the play button is a refined invitation.
- Typography must be intentional — continue the Fraunces + DM Sans pairing established in Folds 1 and 3.
- The video poster image must be dark, moody, and on-brand — treated heavily to match the wine-dark atmosphere.
- Mobile-first: design for 375px, then enhance for desktop.
- The fold must pass the "screenshot test" — even paused (poster state), it should look like a portfolio piece.

### The Decorative Corner Accents

The four corners of the frame get tiny gold "L-shaped" marks — 8px horizontal + 8px vertical lines at each corner. This is a cinematic detail borrowed from film projection and photographic contact sheets. It appears only on desktop (`hidden lg:block`) where the frame is large enough to showcase the detail. On mobile, the clean rounded frame is sufficient.

### The Animated Pulse Ring

Around the play button, a subtle outer ring with `animate-[pulse_3s_ease-in-out_infinite] scale-125` creates a breathing effect — as if the button is quietly inviting. The animation is slow (3s) and restrained. Not a bouncing call-for-attention — a gentle pulse of presence.

Add this to the component's `<style>` block if needed:
```css
@keyframes pulse {
  0%, 100% { opacity: 0.3; transform: scale(1.25); }
  50% { opacity: 0.6; transform: scale(1.35); }
}
```

### Video Hover State (desktop only)

When the user hovers over the entire frame (before clicking play):
- The play button scales up: `group-hover:scale-110`
- The play button background intensifies: `group-hover:bg-[#1A0F10]/80`
- The play button border brightens: `group-hover:border-[#BF8C60]/60`

- Quick transition: `duration-150`
- The poster touch target is the entire frame (the `<button>` fills `inset-0`)

## Animation & Motion Specification

This fold uses **scroll-triggered entrance animations**, consistent with Fold 3. Elements animate in as they enter the viewport.

### Scroll-Triggered Pattern

```jsx
const sectionRef = useRef(null);
const isInView = useInView(sectionRef, { once: true, amount: 0.2 });
```

Trigger at `amount: 0.2` (20% of section in view) — earlier than Fold 3 because the video frame is larger and we want the entrance to begin before the visitor fully scrolls into the fold.

### Element-by-Element Animation

| Element | Initial | Animate (on inView) | Duration | Delay | Easing |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| "— ASSISTA —" label | `opacity: 0, y: 15` | `opacity: 1, y: 0` | 700ms | 0ms | `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` |
| Lead-in headline | `opacity: 0, y: 30` | `opacity: 1, y: 0` | 900ms | 150ms | `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` |
| Video frame | `opacity: 0, y: 40, scale: 0.98` | `opacity: 1, y: 0, scale: 1` | 1100ms | 400ms | `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` |
| Corner accents (each) | `opacity: 0` | `opacity: 1` | 600ms | 1000ms | `easeOut` |
| Whisper metadata | `opacity: 0, y: 15` | `opacity: 1, y: 0` | 700ms | 800ms | `[0.22, 1, 0.36, 1]` |

The frame's entrance is the signature moment — `scale: 0.98 → 1.0` combined with `y: 40 → 0` creates the feeling of the frame "settling into place" like a photograph being hung on a wall.

### Playback Transition

When the user clicks to play, use `AnimatePresence` to transition from poster to iframe:

```jsx
<AnimatePresence mode="wait">
  {!isPlaying ? (
    <motion.div
      key="poster"
      exit={{ opacity: 0, scale: 1.02 }}
      transition={{ duration: 0.4, ease: [0.22, 1, 0.36, 1] }}
    >
      {/* poster content */}
    </motion.div>
  ) : (
    <motion.div
      key="player"
      initial={{ opacity: 0, scale: 0.98 }}
      animate={{ opacity: 1, scale: 1 }}
      transition={{ duration: 0.5, delay: 0.1, ease: [0.22, 1, 0.36, 1] }}
    >
      {/* iframe */}
    </motion.div>
  )}
</AnimatePresence>
```

The poster fades and scales up slightly on exit (suggesting it's "dissolving away"), while the iframe fades in with a subtle scale-up. Quick, cinematic, never jarring.

### `prefers-reduced-motion` Respect

If `prefersReducedMotion` is true:
- Skip all entrance animations (render at final state)
- Skip the pulse ring animation on the play button
- Skip the corner accent delayed fade-in (show them immediately)
- Keep click-to-play transition instant (no animation)

```jsx
const prefersReducedMotion = typeof window !== 'undefined' 
  ? window.matchMedia('(prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)').matches 
  : false;
```

## Atmospheric & Visual Effects

The atmosphere supports the video frame without competing with it.

### Atmospheric Layer Stack (absolute, pointer-events-none, z-0)

**Layer A — Wine-red ambient (behind video frame position):**
`absolute top-1/2 left-1/2 -translate-x-1/2 -translate-y-1/2 w-[700px] h-[500px] lg:w-[1000px] lg:h-[600px]` with inline style `background: radial-gradient(ellipse at center, rgba(166,44,33,0.08) 0%, transparent 65%)`
A subtle wine-red glow positioned exactly where the video frame sits. When the frame is in view, the ambient color bleeds around it — suggesting the frame is emitting warmth. At 0.08 opacity, it's felt more than seen.

**Layer B — Clay warmth side wash (right edge):**
`absolute top-1/4 right-0 w-[400px] h-[400px]` with inline style `background: radial-gradient(ellipse at 80% 50%, rgba(140,61,32,0.05) 0%, transparent 60%)`
An oak-barrel reflection on the right side, adding asymmetric warmth that prevents the fold from feeling too symmetrical or balanced.

**Layer C — Gold projector glow (top edge, wide and thin):**
`absolute top-0 left-0 right-0 h-[200px]` with inline style `background: radial-gradient(ellipse at 50% 0%, rgba(191,140,96,0.06) 0%, transparent 70%)`
A wide, flat gold glow at the top of the section — evokes the light leaking from a film projector positioned behind the viewer. This is the fold's signature atmospheric touch: it subtly reinforces the "private screening room" metaphor.

Procedural SVG noise, consistent with Folds 1 and 3:

### Video Frame Shadow

The video frame itself casts a deep shadow that grounds it in the darkness:
- `shadow-[0_30px_80px_-20px_rgba(0,0,0,0.8)]`

This is a long, soft, dark shadow (not a hard dropshadow). The `-20px` in the spread radius pulls the shadow inward, making it feel like the frame is floating slightly above the background — not pasted onto it.
